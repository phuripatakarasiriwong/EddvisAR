Sydney, Australia

  • Some of the world’s best restaurants, a vibrant arts and music scene, a thriving financial center.

Munich, Germany

  • Munich is a Bavarian metropolis that offers a high quality of life to those who decide to stay a little longer.

Auckland, New Zealand

  • The fact that the city’s oldest park, the Auckland Domain, is built around the eruption crater of an extinct volcano is indicative of what makes Auckland special.

Vienna, Austria

  • Vienna is well known for its centuries-old history as the epicenter of European intellectual life, home to a long list of monumental figures, from Mozart to Freud to Hayek.

Brussels

  • With its beer once consumed as an alternative to dirty drinking water.

Munich

  • Home to Oktoberfest—the renowned drunken festival.

Rome

  • Long considered a bastion of wine, Italy is experiencing a beer breakthrough, where you should go to indulge.

Montpelier, Vermont

  • Montpelier places you in closest proximity to imbibe these treasured beverages.

Cleveland, Ohio

  • An impressive array of award-winning India pale ales might just crown Cleveland the country’s new hop capital.

St. Louis, Missouri

  • Perennial Artisan Ales draws international attention for their annual release of Barrel-Aged Abraxas.

Wellington, New Zealand

  • Garage Project provides one anyway, featuring some of the most spectacular beer in the world.

Asheville, North Carolina

  • Nestled in the Smoky Mountains, Asheville is by no means an undiscovered craft beer destination.

Hood River, Oregon

  • A must for the beer-lover’s bucket list.

Bran Castle, Transylvania, Romania

  • Bran Castle, also known as Dracula’s Castle, lies atop a smoky hill in the middle of this eerie landscape.

Bukhansan Mountain, Seoul, Korea

  • Bukhansan National Park offers an amazing natural getaway just north of this bustling metropolis.

Duoro Wine Region, Portugal

  • One of Portugal’s main wine regions, and a unique place for fall foliage.

Fragrant Hills, Beijing, China

  • An imperial garden in northwestern Beijing that dates back to the 12th century.

Hraunfossar Waterfalls, Husafell, Iceland

  • Stretches for nearly a kilometer, spilling over the giant steps of an ancient lava field into a turquoise glacial river.

Neuschwanstein Castle, Bavaria, Germany

  • Famous for bringing Oktoberfest to the world.

Summer Garden, St. Petersburg, Russia

  • Venice of the North, famous for its gorgeous architecture and alluring parks.

Takachiho Gorge, Miyazaki, Japan

  • The sides of the rock walls go from vertical and smooth to horizontal and jagged and present a natural timeline of the region’s geology.

New Delhi, India

  • Dhanteras typically marks the start of the financial year—and a major shopping season—in India.

Old Delhi, India

  • Rooftops across the old city during Diwali. In Hinduism, the festival celebrates good triumphing over evil.

Kathmandu, Nepal

  • Vendors sell colored powders at a market in Nepal, where the holiday is called Tihar.

Hyderabad, Pakistan

  • Lighting sparklers on the eve of Diwali.

Gandhinagar, India

  • The many oil lamps decorating Akshardham Temple will stay lit until Bhai Duj, the fifth and final day of the festival.

Kolkata, India

  • Fireworks at a park in Kolkata to celebrate Diwali.

Port of Spain, Trinidad

  • Lamps on sale at a market before Diwali celebrations begin—the Caribbean island.

Micato Safaris, Kenya and Tanzania

  • The African sun painting the land in reds and oranges while casting silhouettes over the savannah wildlife.

Angkor Watt, Cambodia

  • The iconic, spired temple complex.

Quebec City, Canada

  • The sun brushstroke the UNESCO World Heritage-designated Old Town in pinks and purples, a live watercolor painting.

Anantara Dhigu Maldives Resort, Maldives

  • Heavenly overwater hammock, where you can sway across the horizon, marooned in the remote shallows of the archipelago.

Salar de Atacama, Atacama Desert, Chile

  • Skies alternate between blue and purple, reflecting off the shallow water.

Aqua Expeditions, Peru

  • Mind-blowing sunsets that create an ever-changing kaleidoscope of colors.

Qasr al Sarab Resort by Anantara, Abu Dhabi

  • Sky-high, rust-hued sand dunes put on a constant spectacle, changing colors and shape with the wind and movement of the sun.

Tradewind Aviation, U.S. and Caribbean

  • Opt for the early evening flights when the pilots of these low-flying planes treat passengers to dreamy sunsets over Boston.

St. Barths, French West Indies

  • Extravagance to the island’s famed jaw-dropping sunsets

Makanyane Safari Lodge, South Africa

  • The secluded suites have floor-to-ceiling glass windows perfect for watching passing elephants.

Giraffe Manor, Nairobi, Kenya

  • Six bedrooms and offers guests the chance to share their breakfast table with the eight resident giraffes.

Lapa Rios Eco Lodge,Islita,Guanacaste,Costa Rica

  • Monkeys, macaws, sloths, and toucans are just a few of the guests you’ll share your stay with.

Sea Lion Lodge, Sea Lion Island, Falkland Islands

  • A rookery of Gentoo penguins has been known to set up camp next to Sea Lion Lodge, a cozy 3-star hotel in the Falkland Islands.

Sarova Salt Lick Game Lodge, Tsavo, Kenya

  • Perched above a watering hole. Spotting wildlife from your room window is possible at any hour of the day.

State Game Lodge, Custer State Park, South Dakota

  • 71,000 acres of nature…and 1,300 free-roaming bison.

Royal Malewane Private Game Lodge, Hoedspruit, South Africa

  • Private plunge pools that come with each room. While soaking in the South African sun, an elephant or two just might drop by.

Valdez, Alaska

  • Surrounded by the glaciated Chugach Mountains and can see as much as 325 inches of the white stuff annually.

Aomori City, Tōhoku, Japan

  • Pummeled with a tremendous 300 average inches of snow a year.

Sapporo, Hokkaido, Japan

  • One of the world’s snowiest. Also famous for producing the Japanese beer.

Quebec City, Quebec, Canada

  • Plenty of ways to celebrate its status as a winter wonderland, with nearly 150 inches of snow falling annually.

Toyama, Hokuriku, Japan

  • More than 143 inches of snow per year.

St. John's, Newfoundland and Labrador, Canada

  • The city is known as the foggiest, the windiest, and the cloudiest of major Canadian cities.

Syracuse, NY

  • The city is well-equipped to deal with the roughly 120 inches of snow it sees annually.

Erie, Pennsylvania

  • With an average of 100 inches of snow falling each year, Erie has cold winters typical of towns in its surrounding snow belt.
